03Problem — root cause

The lesson existed. Nobody could query it.

Where design knowledge lives today

  • Excel trouble reports, PowerPoint design reviews, scanned PDFs
  • Veteran engineers' heads — and they are retiring
  • △ revision histories inside CAD files — "△1: plate 8→10 mm, stress concentration at fastening points"

Why the △ marks matter

The △ history is the densest failure record a company owns — written by the person who fixed the part, on the exact geometry that failed.

And it has never been queryable. It sits in drawings, invisible at the one moment it matters: while the next designer is editing.

Search tools don't help — the designer doesn't know there is something to search for.

11Appendix — anticipated Q&A

Three questions we expect.

How is this different from Glean / CoLab / CADDi?

They index knowledge someone already formalized — standards, documents, drawings — and they only read. We compile unformalized knowledge (△ notes, trouble reports) into executable checks, and we write new knowledge at design time via the flywheel. LLMs made the unformalized→executable conversion possible for the first time — that's the opening.

Can it integrate with real CAD?

Yes — that is the production path. CATIA, NX and SolidWorks all expose add-in APIs that see edit events, which is exactly the hook SenpAI needs. Today's web 3D viewer exists so judges can touch the experience in a browser; the interrupt logic is CAD-agnostic and deterministic.

Where does the data come from?

Three sources the customer already owns: △ revision histories in drawings (link keys), the trouble reports and review PPTs they point to (knowledge body), and — going forward — designers' one-tap confirmations captured at edit time. Today's demo runs on synthetic data modeled on real recurrence patterns; no confidential data is used.
