The lesson existed, but nobody saw it during the edit.

Knowledge is scattered across Excel, PPT, PDF, people's heads, and △ revision notes. Veterans retire. The why disappears. Observed inside a real automotive design floor.

Solution Approach

The knowledge was never missing.
It just could not be executed.

Existing tools wait for knowledge to be searched or formalized into standards. But real design rationale is scattered across drawings, reports, people, and past decisions.

LLMs change that. G-brain retrieves company evidence, and the LLM decides whether this edit deserves an interruption.

The shift: the Company Brain does not just answer questions. It notices risk while you design.

Product Overview

A senpai
watching your screen.

The moment you make a risky edit, the AI interrupts with evidence.

  • Which past △ revision this matches
  • Which document is the source
  • Who solved it last time
  • What happened when it recurred

90-second demo: warning → evidence → recommendation → write-back.

Not a chatbot. Not search. A living Company Brain that interrupts with evidence.

Technology

A Company Brain that lets the LLM
notice risk in context.

01 · OBSERVE

Design event stream

CAD edits become semantic events: part, region, parameter, intent, and surrounding design context.

02 · RETRIEVE

G-brain retrieval

△ notes, reports, owners, past failures, and design rationale are pulled from the Company Brain as evidence.

03 · REASON

LLM risk trigger

The LLM decides whether to interrupt, grounded only in retrieved evidence, and shows the receipts.

Not a brittle rule list. The trigger is an evidence-grounded LLM call over the living Company Brain.
